User Interface

  • Mouse Interaction: Left-click and mouse dragging rotates the view around the world origin.   The view always looks at the origin.  Left-right motion rotates the view around the world y-axis.   Up-down motion rotates the view around the view x-axis.  A limit is implemented at the top and bottom to prevent rotating completely through y-axis (i.e. north/south pole).
  • Key Interaction: The scene contains 1 point source light plus ambient lighting.  The light source can be rotated around the world origin (0,0,0) at a fixed distance from the origin using the following keys:
    1. Keys ‘a’ and ‘d’ should rotate around world y-axis.
    2. Keys ‘s’ and ‘w’ should rotate around world x-axis.
    3. Keys ‘q’ and ‘e’ should rotate around world z-axis.

The Project Requirements

The 3D scene contains 96 spheres.  They are arranged on the faces of an invisible cube with each face containing 16 spheres arranged in 4 x 4 grid.   The spheres on each face vary in their material properties in particular ways.

  1. Mouse Interaction: Left-click and mouse dragging rotates the view around the world origin.   The view always looks at the origin.  Left-right motion rotates the view around the world y-axis.   Up-down motion rotates the view around the view x-axis.  A limit is implemented at the top and bottom to prevent rotating completely through y-axis (i.e. north/south pole).
  2. Key Interaction: The scene contains 1 point source light plus ambient lighting.  The light source can be rotated around the world origin (0,0,0) at a fixed distance from the origin using the following keys:
    1. Keys ‘a’ and ‘d’ should rotate around world y-axis.
    2. Keys ‘s’ and ‘w’ should rotate around world x-axis.
    3. Keys ‘q’ and ‘e’ should rotate around world z-axis.
  3. 3d Scene: The 3D scene contains 96 spheres.  They are be arranged on the faces of an invisible cube with each face containing 16 spheres arranged in 4 x 4 grid.   All spheres are   the same size.  The spheres on a given face should roughly rest on the invisible cube’s face (see Figure (B)) so spheres on one face do not intersect those on an adjacent face by too much. Spheres will vary in their material properties.  Some of these properties are set in the requirements below.  You can choose the remaining aspects of the material properties.
    1. Front Face – the 4 x 4 spheres’ material properties must be as follows:
      1. zero specular reflection
      2. zero diffuse reflection
      3. varying ambient reflection. 
      The ambient reflection coefficient should vary from 0 to 1 as you move across the 4 columns of spheres.   The each row should differ in what common base color the spheres in that row.   The color choice is up to the student.
    2. Right Face – the 4 x 4 spheres’ material properties are as follows:
      1. zero specular reflection
      2. zero ambient reflection 
      3. varying diffuse reflection.
      The diffuse reflection coefficient should vary from 0 to 1 as you move across the 4 columns of spheres.   The each set of spheres in a given row share a common base color but different rows have a different base color.   The color choice is up to the student.
    3. Back Face - the 4 x 4 spheres’ material properties are as follows:
      1. no specular reflection
      2. varying ambient reflection
      3. varying diffuse reflection. 
      The diffuse reflection coefficient should vary from 0 to 1 as you move across the 4 columns of spheres. The ambient reflection coefficient should vary from 0 to 1 as you move up the 4 rows of spheres.   All 16 spheres should use the same common base color.  (Otherwise too many visual properties are varying and it is hard to understand what is changing).
    4. Bottom Face - the 4 x 4 spheres’ material properties are as follows:
      1. a fixed, non-zero amount of ambient reflection 
      2. a fixed, non-zero amount of diffuse reflection 
      3. varying amount of specular reflection.
      The specular reflection coefficient should vary from 0 to 1 as you move across the 4 columns of spheres. The specular exponent coefficient should vary from 0 to 1 as you move up across different rows of spheres.   All spheres should use the same common base color.  (The color choice is up to the student).  Choose a fixed ambient and diffuse reflection amount so that the specular highlights and there variations are visible.
    5. Left Face  - the 4 x 4 spheres’ material properties are as follows:
      1. a fixed amount of ambient reflection
      2. fixed amount of diffuse reflection
      3. no specular reflection 
      4. varying emission coefficients. 
      The student can choice whatever the colors.  At least 4 of the spheres must emit their own light.
    6. Top Face– the 4 x 4 spheres’ material properties are as follows:
      1. The student can vary material properties in way desired (color, diffuse, specular, emission, etc.).
